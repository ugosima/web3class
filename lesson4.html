Why Is Crypto even accepted as money?

Ultimately, money is a shared belief; If a high number of people believes that a particular material that has certain properties is a medium of exchange, then that material becomes "Money".

However, there are basic properties of Money, Cryptocurrency (like Bitcoin or Ethereum) is accepted as money because it has these properties, though digital and decentralized. For a blockchain to function, it needs its validators , and for its validators to keep working, they need to be payed; This is where the concept of cryptocurrency was born, cryptocurrencies are digital currencies that are used as a system of reward for nodes(people) validating a blockchain, and as a means of payment by people that are using the blockchain; This payment algorithm is wired into the blockchain at creation and becomes part of the system forever; There is a common mistake of trying to visualize cryptocurrencies, which is why it is important to emphasize that cryptocurrencies are not physical objects but rather recorded data that can be passed from one person to another; A simple abstract analogy is that cryptocurrencies are like entitlements recorded on the blockchain, you can not touch them, but everybody knows that you are the only person entitled to them. Now a blockchain can be built by anybody for many reasons, however if you want to use a blockchain that you did not build, you will need to pay for the services of that blockchain, and you will need to pay in the blockchain's native cryptocurrency. So here's the mental model, if you want to use a blockchain, you need to pay for it, and you can only pay for it with the blockchain's cryptocurrency, so you need to buy the cryptocurrency, and to buy the cryptocurrency, you need to use money, so you exchange your money for the cryptocurrency, and then you can use the blockchain. And as many more people use the blockchain, more people need to buy the cryptocurrency, and as more people buy the cryptocurrency, if the supply is limited, the value of the cryptocurrency increases; This is the basic meaning of utility, which today of course is a serious driving force for crypto prices. The properties of a cryptocurrency that determines its total supply, mode of validator reward, behaviours and how it is used, are all determined at the creation of the blockchain, and they are part of the system forever; This is why cryptocurrencies are often compared to gold, because they have a limited supply and are not controlled by any central authority, which makes them a good store of value and a hedge against inflation. You might want to ask who and who uses these blockchains, and the answer is that there are many different types of blockchains, some are public and open to anyone, while others are private and only accessible to certain organizations or individuals; Some blockchains are used for financial transactions, while others are used for supply chain management, voting systems, digital identity, government, or even social media platforms; The use cases for blockchains are diverse because they provide a secure, transparent, and decentralized way to record and verify data, which can be applied in many different industries. Even if you are not part of a company, you are a user of the blockchain everytime you use cryptocurrency, and you make your payment to the validators by paying gas fees(A small fee paid to the validators for processing your transaction). This is one of the main reasons why cryptocurrencies are accepted as money, because they are needed to use the services of their respective blockchains, and as more people use those blockchains, more people need to use the cryptocurrency, which increases its acceptance and value. To be accepted as money, an item must have these properties or functions :

1. Medium of exchange People must be able to use it to buy goods and services.
2. Store of Value It must keep its value over time so people can save it.
3. Unit of Account Prices must be able to be expressed in it .
4. Divisibility It must be easily divided into smaller parts.
5. Portability It must be easy to carry and transfer .
6. Durability It must withstand wear and tear.
7. Fungibility Each unit must be interchangeable (just as a one dollar bill is the same as another).
10. Acceptability People must generally agree to accept it as payment.
11. Limited Supply There should be a controlled supply to prevent inflation .
12. Security It must be secure against counterfeiting.
13. Accessibility It should be easy for people to access and use .

These functions are why we accept things like gold, silver, or even paper money as "money", because they allow us to trade, save, and measure value. Cryptocurrency meets these functions in many ways, and is largely accepted because:


  1. People trust it's Technology : Crypto is secured by blockchain technology and cryptography; This makes it tamper-proof, transparent, and decentralized. The world accepts it because the rules are clear, secure, and enforced by code, not banks or governments.
  2. It is borderless and fast : With Crypto,
    1. People can send money to anyone, anywhere in the world.
    2. Transactions are fast and often cost less than traditional methods.
    3. You don’t need a bank or even government permission.

    This makes it very useful for international trade, remittances, and online payments, and is especially useful in countries with unstable currencies and/or limited banking access.
  3. Limited Supply : Bitcoin for example, has a maximum supply of 21 million coins; Scarcity makes it similar to gold, which people trust to hold value over time.
  4. People Control Their Money : Cryptocurrency gives users ownership and control over their funds without needing banks or governments. This means:
    1. No one can freeze your account.
    2. No one can stop your transaction.
    3. No inflation from central banks printing more.

    This financial freedom is very appealing to many people, especially in places with corrupt or unstable governments.

How did people come to believe that Cryptocurrency Is money?


People came to believe that cryptocurrency is money over time due to a combination of economic behavior, utility, technological expansion, and social consensus; Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies began fulfilling the roles traditional money played because people used them to buy goods, saved them as investments, and even priced items. people trusted cryptocurrencies because they are built on blockchain technology, which is secure, transparent, and decentralized. This means that their investments were safe and hassle free. In regions with unstable banks or hyperinflation, crypto became a safe alternative.

As more people and institutions began to buy, use, and accept crypto, a belief formed: "If others treat it like money, maybe I should too." This social proof helped drive adoption, and now for many, crypto is not just digital money, it is now a strong movement and a generally important aspect of finance and technology.
